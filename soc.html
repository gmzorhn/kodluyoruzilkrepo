Sociologists Information

Auguste Comte

Auguste Comte (1798-1857) is often regarded as the father of sociology. He introduced the term "sociology" and emphasized the scientific study of society.

Karl Marx

Karl Marx (1818-1883) was a German philosopher and economist whose ideas about class struggle and social change have had a profound impact on sociology.

Georg Simmel

Georg Simmel (1858-1918) was a German sociologist known for his work on social forms, social interaction, and the philosophy of money.

Émile Durkheim

Émile Durkheim (1858-1917) was a French sociologist who established sociology as a formal academic discipline and studied social facts, collective conscience, and social integration.

Max Weber

Max Weber (1864-1920) was a German sociologist known for his work on bureaucracy, social action, and the relationship between religion and capitalism.
